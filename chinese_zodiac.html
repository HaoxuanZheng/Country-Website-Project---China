Chinese Zodiac

Each year in the zodiac cycle corresponds to an animal or mythological figure in Chinese folklore.

Chinese Zodiac

The Legend of the Chinese Zodiac: According to Chinese mythology, the Jade Emperor, who ruled over Heaven and Earth, decided to hold a race to determine the order of the zodiac animals. He invited all the animals to participate, and the first 12 to cross the finish line would be honored with a spot in the zodiac.

Chinese Zodiac

What do the Chinese zodiac animals represent?

The Chinese zodiac is a yearly classification system in Chinese culture that follows the Chinese lunar lunisolar calendar (literally meaning "heaven and earth"), in which each year is assigned an animal for a 12-year cycle, and most animals are associated with a personality that represents that year and the person born in that year.
